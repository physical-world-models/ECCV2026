From Video World Models as Data Engines to World Action Models for Embodied Decision-Making.

About the Workshop

Physical World Models are emerging as the scaling infrastructure for Physical AI. This workshop is organized around two tightly connected directions: scalable physical data engines and embodied decision-making models.

Direction I explores Physical World Models as scalable data engines: omnimodal world models that unify real videos, robot demonstrations, simulation rollouts, egocentric observations, digital twins, operation logs, and tactile/force/proprioceptive traces into scalable physical experience. Topics include Real2Sim2Real data loops, geometry-aware 3D/4D world generation, generative physical experience, vision-tactile data pipelines, and Physical AI data scaling laws.

Direction II focuses on World Action Models for embodied decision-making: predictive models that jointly reason about future states, actions, values, risks, and task progress. Topics include JEPA-style latent world action models, generative and joint WAMs, vision-tactile WAMs for fine manipulation, closed-loop propose-predict-evaluate-act-replan control, and deployment-oriented embodied foundation models for complex robotic tasks.

Topics of Interest

Topics include, but are not limited to:

Direction I — Physical World Models as Scalable Data Engines

From fragmented physical traces to scalable, controllable, robot-ready experience.
  • Omnimodal world models that unify real videos, robot demonstrations, simulation rollouts, egocentric observations, digital twins, operation logs, and tactile/force/proprioceptive traces into scalable physical experience
  • Real2Sim2Real data loops that lift real-world observations into editable 3D/4D digital twins, generate controllable rollouts and curricula, and feed deployment traces back into world-model refinement
  • Geometry-aware 3D/4D world generation for robot-usable states, including object-centric scene graphs, dynamic geometry, affordances, occlusions, contact regions, and uncertainty
  • Generative physical experience for policy learning: synthetic demonstrations, pseudo-trajectories, action-conditioned futures, reset states, rewards, labels, failure cases, and long-tail scenarios
  • Vision-tactile world-model data pipelines that generate synchronized visual, tactile, force/torque, proprioceptive, and action trajectories for learning contact-rich physical interactions
  • Physical AI data scaling laws: how model performance scales with embodiment diversity, task diversity, interaction density, multimodal sensing, and generated-versus-real physical experience

Direction II — World Action Models for Embodied Decision-Making

From reactive policies to predictive, value-aware, closed-loop embodied action.
  • World Action Models that jointly model future states, actions, values, risks, and task progress, moving embodied agents beyond reactive VLA policies toward predictive decision-making
  • JEPA-style latent world action models for efficient action-conditioned representation prediction, abstract rollout, image/language-goal planning, and long-horizon search
  • Generative and joint WAMs that couple future visual/tactile/proprioceptive states with latent actions, continuous actions, value estimation, and sampling-based planning
  • Vision-tactile WAMs for fine manipulation, modeling contact transitions, slip, friction, force evolution, deformation, tactile feedback, and corrective micro-actions
  • Closed-loop embodied control via propose-predict-evaluate-act-replan, connecting world models with MPC, value-guided planning, uncertainty-aware control, and failure recovery
  • Deployment-oriented embodied foundation models for dexterous manipulation, bimanual coordination, tool use, mobile manipulation, autonomous driving, HRI, and industrial automation

Call for Papers

We invite original, unpublished work on physical world models for scaling Physical AI.

  • Scope: Physical World Models as scalable data engines; World Action Models for embodied decision-making.
  • Format: up to 4 pages, excluding references, using the IROS workshop template.
  • Review: double-blind; non-archival unless otherwise specified.
  • Criteria: relevance, originality, technical quality, clarity, and impact on Physical AI scaling.
  • Presentation: accepted papers as posters (~20), with selected oral spotlights (~6).
  • Submission types: technical papers, position papers, datasets, benchmarks, challenge reports, and negative results.
  • Artifacts: datasets, simulators, evaluation suites, code, and reproducible baselines are encouraged.

Submission deadline: August 10, 2026  ·  Notification: August 20, 2026  ·  Camera-ready: aligned with the IROS workshop schedule (TBA)

Dates are tentative and may be adjusted. Submissions will be handled via OpenReview (link coming soon).
